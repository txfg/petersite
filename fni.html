02
Risk & Reliability · Texas River Authority

Canal Failure Prediction System

The Journey

I'm building a risk-prediction system for a major Texas river authority's canal network from the ground up — starting from the first principle of risk itself: Risk = Likelihood × Consequence.

From there I define every factor we believe drives likelihood and every factor that drives consequence — deliberately stopping before the model gets so granular it stops being useful. The system resolves those factors into a single risk surface and renders it as a heat map overlaid on the authority's GIS data, so the highest-priority Potential Failure Modes (PFMs) surface exactly where they sit on the network.

"Most river-system failures are unforeseen — by the time there's a visible sign, the cheap fix is already gone."

The Struggle & The Result

River systems fail expensively. Failures tend to show signs only once it's already too late to address them cheaply, turning routine maintenance into emergency repair. By surfacing where failure is both most likely and most costly before it happens, the system shifts budget from reactive repair to targeted prevention.

03
Risk Mitigation · Claims & Communication

Email Practices as Legal Liability

The Journey

I analyzed a claim brought against the firm that traced back to poor email practices — long, stringy threads that kept growing without anyone stepping back to read them as a whole.

"What the team intended as a private, helpful debate with the client became the single strongest exhibit against us."

The Struggle & The Result

A lengthy email string is a documented conversation — and a documented conversation can be used against you. In a recent claim, a contractor obtained our client emails through discovery and turned our own words into a central argument against our case.

So I turned the post-mortem into an educational program built to reach all 1,500 people at the firm: healthy communication practices delivered across formats — a presentation rebuilt on FNI's official template, live meetings, and group sessions — so the lesson actually changes behavior instead of sitting in a folder.
